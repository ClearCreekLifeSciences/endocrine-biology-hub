🧬 MOTS‑c (Subcutaneous) — Educational Overview
Overview

MOTS‑c (Mitochondrial Open Reading Frame of the 12S rRNA type‑c) is a 16‑amino‑acid peptide encoded within mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA). It belongs to a class of peptides known as mitochondrial‑derived peptides (MDPs), which function as metabolic regulators and cellular stress‑response signals.

Unlike most peptides encoded in nuclear DNA, MOTS‑c originates from the mitochondrial genome and is expressed in response to metabolic demand, nutrient stress, and cellular energy imbalance.

Research literature describes MOTS‑c as influencing pathways related to AMPK activation, glucose utilization, fatty‑acid oxidation, and cellular homeostasis.

Physiological Context

MOTS‑c is naturally expressed in:

Skeletal muscle

Liver

Adipose tissue

Circulating plasma

Its expression is associated with:

Cellular energy sensing

Metabolic adaptation

Exercise‑induced mitochondrial signaling

Stress‑response pathways

MOTS‑c is one of several mitochondrial‑encoded peptides (along with humanin and SHLPs) that act as intracellular and endocrine‑like messengers.

Receptor Signaling and Mechanistic Pathways

Current research identifies several mechanistic themes:

AMPK activation: MOTS‑c activates AMP‑activated protein kinase, a central regulator of cellular energy balance

Metabolic reprogramming: Promotes glucose uptake and glycolytic flux under metabolic stress

Folate‑dependent pathways: Interacts with the folate‑methionine cycle, influencing one‑carbon metabolism

Stress‑response signaling: Enhances cellular resilience during oxidative or metabolic stress

Exercise‑linked effects: Endogenous MOTS‑c levels increase during physical activity, suggesting a role in exercise adaptation

These pathways position MOTS‑c as a subject of interest in metabolic, mitochondrial, and aging‑related research.

Scientific and Regulatory Context

Published mitochondrial and metabolic research describes MOTS‑c as:

A mitochondrial‑encoded peptide with endocrine‑like signaling properties

A regulator of cellular energy homeostasis and AMPK‑related pathways

A molecule involved in stress adaptation, exercise physiology, and metabolic flexibility

A peptide with ongoing investigation in metabolic and aging biology
