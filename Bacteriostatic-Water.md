💧 Bacteriostatic Water — Educational Overview

Overview

Bacteriostatic Water for Injection (BWFI) is sterile water containing 0.9% benzyl alcohol as a preservative. It is widely used in research and compounding settings as a diluent for reconstituting lyophilized (freeze-dried) peptides, proteins, and other substances supplied in powder form.

Unlike Sterile Water for Injection (which contains no preservative and is intended for single-use), bacteriostatic water's benzyl alcohol content inhibits the growth of most common bacteria, allowing a reconstituted vial to be used multiple times over a longer period when handled properly.

Bacteriostatic water is not itself a peptide, drug, or active compound — it is a reconstitution vehicle.

Physiological/Compositional Context

Composition: Sterile water for injection, USP, with 0.9% benzyl alcohol added as a bacteriostatic preservative

Benzyl alcohol's role: An aromatic alcohol that exerts antimicrobial activity by disrupting bacterial cell membranes, extending in-use viability of a multi-dose vial

Osmolarity: Approximately isotonic due to the benzyl alcohol content, distinguishing it from plain sterile water

Appearance/stability: Clear, colorless liquid; should remain visually clear — cloudiness, discoloration, or particulate matter are indicators the solution should be discarded

Cold-storage behavior: Some reconstituted solutions can display transient increased viscosity or a gel-like appearance when stored cold; this is generally a physical cold-viscosity effect that resolves upon warming to room temperature, rather than an indicator of degradation

Handling and Storage Considerations (General/Educational)

Typically stored refrigerated (2–8°C) both before and after reconstitution use

Multi-dose vials are generally intended for a limited in-use window once punctured (commonly cited as ~28 days in clinical multi-dose contexts), after which the vial is discarded regardless of remaining volume

Vial stoppers are designed for repeated needle penetration but should be swabbed with alcohol before each draw to reduce contamination risk

Should never be injected in individuals with a known benzyl alcohol sensitivity

Scientific and Regulatory Context

Bacteriostatic Water for Injection, USP, is an FDA-recognized pharmaceutical-grade diluent listed in the United States Pharmacopeia

It is not a drug or therapeutic agent itself; its regulatory classification is as a diluent/vehicle used in the preparation of other formulations

It appears throughout hospital, compounding pharmacy, and research contexts as the standard reconstitution medium for lyophilized peptides

No therapeutic claims are associated with bacteriostatic water itself; its function is procedural (dissolution/reconstitution), not pharmacological
